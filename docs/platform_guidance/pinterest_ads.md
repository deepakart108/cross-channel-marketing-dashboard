# Pinterest Ads — Reach, Frequency & CTR Benchmarks

Source: Pinterest Business — "Redefining reach: Why it matters for your brand"
(business.pinterest.com/insights/effective-reach), Pinterest Business Media Planner
documentation (help.pinterest.com), Pinterest "Path to Performance Guide"
(business.pinterest.com/pdf/pinterest-presents/path-to-performance)

## Reach and frequency

Pinterest's own guidance frames reach and frequency as a pair: reach delivers the audience,
frequency is how repeatedly that audience is exposed to the ad. Pinterest's Media Planner tool
surfaces estimated weekly frequency alongside impressions, CPM, and reach for Awareness and
Consideration campaigns — weekly frequency is the unit Pinterest itself plans against, which
is why WoW frequency comparisons are the right cadence for fatigue checks on this platform.

## CTR benchmarks

Placement has an outsized effect on Pinterest CTR: ads in **Top of Search** placements saw a
**41% higher CTR**, and ads in the top ten Search/Related-Pins slots saw a **39% higher CTR**
than lower placements in Pinterest's own case studies. A campaign posting well below typical
CTR is more often a placement/targeting mismatch than a raw creative-quality problem on this
platform — worth checking placement mix before assuming the creative itself is the issue.

## Path to Performance

Pinterest's advertiser scorecard (the "Path to Performance" framework) coaches accounts on
signal health (event quality), catalog feed ingestion, and feed health as leading indicators —
underscoring that on Pinterest, shopping/catalog campaigns often trace performance problems
back to feed and signal quality rather than creative alone.
