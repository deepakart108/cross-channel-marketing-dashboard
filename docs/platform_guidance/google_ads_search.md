# Google Search Ads — Quality Score & Impression Share

Source: Google Ads Help — "About Quality Score for Search campaigns"
(support.google.com/google-ads/answer/6167118), "About impression share"
(support.google.com/google-ads/answer/2497703)

## Quality Score

Quality Score is a diagnostic tool (1–10 scale, at the keyword level) showing how an ad's
quality compares to other advertisers. It is **not** an input to the ad auction and Google
explicitly warns it "should not be optimized or aggregated with the rest of your data" — use
it to diagnose, not as a KPI.

It combines three components, each rated "Above average," "Average," or "Below average"
against the last 90 days of other advertisers:

1. **Expected CTR** — the likelihood the ad is clicked when shown.
2. **Ad relevance** — how closely the ad matches the intent behind the search.
3. **Landing page experience** — how relevant and useful the landing page is to people who
   click the ad.

A low CTR on a search campaign is one of the clearest below-average signals for the Expected
CTR component, and typically points to ad copy that doesn't match query intent or targeting
that's too broad for the ad's message.

## Impression share

**Impression share (IS) = impressions / total eligible impressions.** Eligible impressions
are estimated from ad auctions, factoring in targeting, approval status, and quality.

Three things move impression share:
1. **Budget constraints** — insufficient budget limits visibility in eligible auctions.
2. **Quality and bid competitiveness** — a lower Quality Score or an under-competitive bid
   reduces how often the ad clears the auction bar.
3. **Competitive landscape** — more/stronger competitors bidding on the same terms shrinks
   the share available to any one advertiser.

Google notes that "small fluctuations over time don't necessarily indicate that action is
needed" — but a sudden, sizeable drop with **no corresponding budget or bid change** points
toward rising competition or a Quality Score regression rather than a self-inflicted cause.
