## Creative fatigue

Creative fatigue occurs when an audience has seen the same creative too many times and
performance begins to decline as a result. Meta's Ads Manager surfaces creative fatigue
recommendations directly against campaigns showing this pattern.

Frequency benchmarks used by media buyers operating within Meta's guidance:
- **Cold prospecting audiences:** healthy frequency is 1.5–2.5 over a 7-day window; 3.0–4.0
  is a warning zone that often precedes a CTR decline.
- **Retargeting audiences:** healthy frequency is 3.0–5.0 (retargeting audiences tolerate
  more repetition); 6.0+ is a warning zone.
- **Recommended fix:** add 3–5 new creative variations before considering audience expansion
  — refreshing creative resolves fatigue more reliably than widening the audience.

## Ad Relevance Diagnostics

Meta scores every ad on three diagnostics, each relative to other ads competing for the same
audience:

1. **Quality Ranking** — perceived ad quality vs. competing ads, informed by negative
   feedback (hiding/reporting the ad) and low-quality signals such as withheld information,
   sensationalized language, or engagement bait.
2. **Engagement Rate Ranking** — expected engagement rate vs. competing ads.
3. **Conversion Rate Ranking** — expected conversion rate vs. ads with the same optimization
   goal, competing for the same audience.

These diagnostics help distinguish *why* an ad underperforms: a below-average Quality or
Engagement Rate Ranking points to a creative/audience-fit problem, while a below-average
Conversion Rate Ranking with strong engagement points to a landing-page or offer problem.
