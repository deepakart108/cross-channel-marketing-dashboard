# Snapchat Ads — Frequency & Delivery Metrics

Source: Snap Business Help Center — "Delivery Metrics Glossary for Single Image or Video Ads"
and "Ad Performance FAQs" (businesshelp.snapchat.com), Snapchat for Business —
"Snapchat Ads Manager Metrics: How to Define and Monitor KPIs" (forbusiness.snapchat.com)

## Paid frequency

Snap defines **paid frequency** as the average number of times an ad was served to a
Snapchatter, calculated as **paid impressions ÷ paid reach**. Rising frequency with flat reach
is called out explicitly as a warning sign in Snap's own guidance — it means the same
audience is absorbing all the new delivery instead of the campaign finding fresh reach.

## Cost and frequency together

Snap's guidance pairs frequency with CPM rather than looking at either alone: "over-indexing
on impressions while ignoring frequency" is flagged as a common advertiser mistake, since the
same people seeing an ad too many times causes performance to decay even while impression
volume looks healthy. Watching CPM and frequency together is the recommended way to catch this
before it shows up as a CTR or CPA problem.

## Swipe rate (CTR)

Swipe rate is Snap's name for CTR — swipes divided by impressions. As with Meta and TikTok, a
declining swipe rate that coincides with climbing frequency is the platform-native fatigue
signature, not two unrelated metrics moving independently.
