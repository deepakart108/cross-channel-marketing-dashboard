# Programmatic / Display — MRC Viewability Standard

Source: Media Rating Council (MRC) & Interactive Advertising Bureau (IAB) — "MRC Viewable Ad
Impression Measurement Guidelines" (mediaratingcouncil.org, iab.com/guidelines), industry
tracking via digitalapplied.com's 2026 display benchmark aggregation

## The standard

The MRC/IAB viewability standard, introduced in 2014 and still the industry baseline, defines
a "viewable" impression as:

- **Display ads:** at least 50% of pixels visible on screen for at least **1 continuous
  second**.
- **Video ads:** at least 50% of pixels visible for at least **2 continuous seconds**.
- **Large-format ads** (≥242,500 pixels): only 30% of pixels need to be visible to count.

Viewability is measured by MRC-accredited vendors via JavaScript tags or SDKs that track pixel
visibility and time-in-view inside the user's browser viewport — it is a placement/inventory
quality measurement, not a creative or targeting measurement.

## Benchmark context

Cross-network viewability averaged **72% in 2026**, up from 67% in 2024, but format varies
widely: native inventory clears ~81%, CTV/display reaches ~96%, while desktop banner lags at
~64%. A placement sitting well under the ~50% MRC pass threshold — not just below the network
average — indicates an inventory/placement quality problem (e.g., below-the-fold slots, low
quality supply-side partners) rather than a creative or audience-targeting issue, since
viewability is purely about whether the ad had a chance to be seen at all.
